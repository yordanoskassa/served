The problem

The scariest envelope a business owner ever opens.

Millions of subpoenas, garnishment orders, and records demands land on US small businesses every year — most on businesses that aren't even being sued. For immigrant owners, one envelope is three problems at once.

Language

Can't read it

Legal English is a third language even for fluent speakers. "Subpoena duces tecum" says nothing about what to actually do before the deadline.

Trust

Can't tell if it's real

Fake "final legal notice" letters are built to look exactly like real process. The FTC logs thousands of impersonation-scam complaints; immigrant merchants are prime targets.

Stakes

Wrong is catastrophic — both ways

Ignore a real garnishment order and the business can owe the employee's entire debt. Pay a fake notice and the money is gone. A lawyer at $300–500/hr to find out? Thousands.

Our product

AI reads. Rules check. Humans confirm.

Served is not a chatbot guessing about your document. It's a verification pipeline: every claim is checked against the actual court record before you see it, and high-stakes steps go through a human attorney.
